Project Introduction


Real-time Tracking and Analyzing System for Soccer Games

About

background

Soccer is one of the most popular sports in the world and soccer game analysis has always been a hot spot for soccer lovers. Nowadays, with the rapid development of computer science and technology, automatic analysis systems have already been designed and implemented in some countries in order to reduce the workload and labor cost. However, as modern soccer industry is still in infancy in China, there isn't a very mature and well-developed analysis system in our market so far. As a result, our research group is aimed to develop a real-time system to help track players' movements under multi-view videos and automaticlly analyze their performance in a soccer game. This is a cooperation project with Shanghai Sports School and our system is planned to be applied in their future teaching activities.

My Work.

team leader of tracking algorithm group

I am the team leader of tracking algorithm group and my major work focus on soccer players tracking and ball detection task. At the beginning of our research, we chose Spatio-Temporal Context Learning [1] to achieve the tracking in MATLAB. In order to avoid negative effect of occlusions and improve accuracy, we also combined video information from different views. Then, considering that spatio-temporal context is more applicable in complex scenes and MATLAB has a relatively low speed, we turned to develop a C++ & openCV program based on Matching Summary Representation [2]. Nevertheless, ball detection is always a challenging task, as the ball is too small in long shot vedio and its features vary over frames. So I used information in HSV space to help raise accuracy of ball detection. Recently, I am continuing trying to make progress in both speed and performance.

Contributions

MATLAB version based on Spatio-Temporal Context Learning

Spatio-Temporal Context Learning is an effective and robust tracking algorithm which exploits the spatio-temporal context for visual tracking. The approach takes advantage of the relationship between the local scenes containing the object in consecutive frames and realizes the tracking by computing a confidence map. Source code that is implemented in MATLAB and can detect one walking man is avaliable online. However, I made some adjustment to the original code and achieved the tracking of more than one person. Moreover, I took the video information from different pespectives into consideration and as a result, even if there exist occultions in one perspective, we may still track players correctly with videos of other views, thus avoiding the loss of target. Besides, I also developed a simple demo using MATLAB GUI to demonstate our idea of the system. As the left image shows, the demo system takes video frames of two perspectives as input and prints a corresponding plane graph and a trajectory diagram as output. The initial positions are located manually by users.

C++ version based on Matching Summary Representation

Matching Summary Representation(MSR) is a method proposed in book Computer Vision: A Modern Approach. The general idea is that, although the small-scale details such as colors of an individual pixel or the responses of oriented filteres might change from frame to frame, a summary representation of the domain might always hold similarities. A histogram representation is an attractive choice because two histograms will be similar only when the two patches have similar numbers of similar structures in them. So we implemented a C++ program based on MSR to track the players' movements. The speed has become faster than previous MATLAB version based on CT, but the performance is almost the same. Also, the initial positions are located manually by users.

Zoom In

improvement of Ball Detection

Ball detection and tracking is important but also challenging for soccer game analysis as many problems may affect the detection result. However, previously we just used the same algorithm to deal with ball and players, which led to the loss of ball sometimes. Thus, I decided to add some special steps to adjust ball detection. First, I selected a region of interest(ROI) around the detected ball via MSR. Then, in the ROI, the ground is extracted in HSV color space. Next ball candidates are searched in term of size and shape analysis. If the detected ball is close to a ball candidate, the detection is believed to be correct, otherwise, the candidates need to be further examined in detail such as gray level, circularity and distance to determine the optimal object. As a consequence, the lost ball can be detected again.

Plane Graph

To indicate the tracking results more clearly, the reconstruction of a two-dimensional model of the field is necessary. Thus, we need conduct perspective transformation from a video view to plane graph. In MATLAB version, the transformation matrix is computed by function estimateGeometricTransfrom and is used by function transformPointsForward. While in C++ & openCV version, the matrix is computed by function cv::getPerspectiveTransform and is used by function cv::perspectiveTransform. The left image shows an example of the corresponding projection relationship.
